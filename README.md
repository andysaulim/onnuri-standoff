## About

This interactive map traces a four-day maritime confrontation in the Yellow Sea's Provisional Measures Zone (PMZ) — a jointly-managed fishery zone established under the 2001 Korea–China Fisheries Agreement.

In December 2025, South Korea dispatched the research vessel **ONNURI** to inspect Chinese aquaculture structures built inside the PMZ without Korean consultation. China's Coast Guard responded by intercepting and blocking the mission, deploying rubber boats within 1km of the target platform.

The animation is built from live AIS (Automatic Identification System) vessel tracking data, showing the real movements of all seven vessels over the four-day period.

## How to Use

- **Play / Pause** — click the ▶ button or press `Space`
- **Scrub** — click anywhere on the progress bar, or use `←` `→` arrow keys
- **Jump to day** — click Dec 3 / 4 / 5 / 6 on the timeline
- **Speed** — click `1×` to cycle through 0.5×, 1×, 2×, 4×
- **Toggle vessels** — click any vessel in the legend to show/hide its track
- **Maximize map** — click ⛶ (top-left of map) or press `M`
- **Story cards** — appear automatically as the animation plays; click to pause

## Background

The PMZ was established in 2001 to manage overlapping Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) claims between South Korea and China in the Yellow Sea. Under the agreement, both sides are supposed to consult before installing permanent structures in the zone.

China has placed three aquaculture platforms — Shen Lan 1, Shen Lan 2, and Atlantic Amsterdam — inside the PMZ without Korean consultation. South Korea's Coast Guard has intercepted Korean monitoring vessels more than 27 times since 2020.
